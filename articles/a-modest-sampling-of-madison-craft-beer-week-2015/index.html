A modest sampling of Madison Craft Beer Week 2015

Kristian Knutsen
@polychronicon /
Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi /
April 28, 2015 @ 12:00 am

events

Star Wars-themed beers, a beer-can derby, and a ridiculous amount of Kentucky Derby parties.

Given that Madison Craft Beer Week, which runs May 1 through 10 and is returning for its fifth year, spans nearly 400 different events at some 80 venues, there's no way that beer and only beer can be the focus of everything. This is an event that increasingly gets to have it both ways: drawing out those folks who fuss over, dissect and sip their way through rare brews and tap takeovers, but also herding in, you know, other people who buy alcohol, and by the dozen. In the interest of narrowing down the overwhelming MCBW schedule, here's a look at a few pop culture-mined or just plain offbeat highlights.

FRIDAY MAY 1

Craftsman Canwood Derby (Craftsman Table & Tap)

The classic Cub Scout experience that is the Pinewood Derby has seen a revival in recent years, with a growing number of taverns hosting these gravity-assisted model-car races. While these rolling racers are typically constructed from a block of wood, plastic wheels, and nail-like axles, this derby starts with a different raw material. That's a beer can, of course.

Craftsman Table & Tap in Middleton is hosting its inaugural “canwood derby" for this opening night of Madison Craft Beer Week. In it, contestants will race toy cars built from beer cans down an incline, with the top winner claiming glory and a Founders Brewing longboard.

Cans from five breweries are officially sponsored for a special derby run: Anderson Valley of Boonville, California, Founders of Grand Rapids, Michigan, Nebraska Brewing of Papillion, Nebraska, Oskar Blues of Longmont, Colorado, and Surly of Minneapolis. Kits are available now at Craftsman, and may also be purchased on the day of the race for last-minute entries. (Think about that for a sec—you can buy a kit for a car that you make while you're drunk. Someone's a marketing genius.) For those prospective racers making their own cars at home, each entry must feature an entire American craft beer can, with no macros or imports allowed.

There's a lengthy set of rules and regulations governing the cars' dimensions and weight, and all entries must be inspected and approved prior to taking the track. Check in is at 6 p.m. with heats commencing at 7 p.m. Will it be Furious or G'Knight by a tab?

SATURDAY MAY 2

Kentucky Derby Viewing & Hat Party (The Sett)

The Kentucky Derby and beer are by no means a proper pairing. This august thoroughbred horse race held the first Saturday of each May is all about traditions, and none may be more established than the mint julep. This cocktail combining bourbon, simple syrup and muddled mint is not merely the official drink of the Derby, it's as much an icon of the race as the twin steeples of Churchill Downs. They're served by the tens of thousands to spectators in the stands, and are standard at Derby day watch parties among fans everywhere.

So why are there six (!) Kentucky Derby-themed gatherings at MCBW? Because there's an equally out-of-control rush lately for bourbon barrel-aged beers. Top shelf tickets for serious craft breweries and the fans who covet, hoard and even enjoy them, these beers big in both flavor and ABV are often associated with Kentucky. The state's name is used for craft beers made around the nation, primarily to signify aging in bourbon barrels. So, why not raise such a beer in lieu of a mint julep?

That's the idea at the Sett, the craft beer-friendly big-screen cavern at Union South. This Derby watch party will feature several bourbon barrel-aged brews on tap, and for those who want to really mark the occasion, materials for making your own giant hat. No word on whether flowers or feathers will be available, but they should go well with boozy brews.

Another Kentucky Derby watch party will be hosted by the Echo Tap, which will serve beers from New Orleans-based Abita and award two $50 gift cards to the man and woman bedecked in their old Kentucky finest. The High Noon Saloon will also be hosting a celebration, complete with a barrel of Kentucky Breakfast Stout (KBS) from Founders and classic western swing by the North Country Drifters. Likewise, Jordan's Big 10 Pub is throwing its own party, this one featuring Green Flash Brewing of San Diego and Southern Tier Brewing of Lakewood, New York. And Gray's Tied House in Verona is putting on “Talk Derby to Me!" with a hat contest and its own Gray's Brewing products.

The biggest MCBW take on the Kentucky Derby, though, may be at the viewing party at the Majestic. Barrel-aged beers are on the menu here, including a trio of vintages from Widmer Brothers, the fleeting Bourbon County Stout from Chicago's Goose Island, and whiskey pairings with the brews.

MONDAY MAY 4

Gourd Vader (Tex Tubb's Taco Palace)

Star Wars Day is a relatively new phenomenon, having only gotten rolling earlier this decade, but it looks set to take off this year. With the recent release of a new “The Force Awakens" trailer at Star Wars Celebration, mania for that galaxy far, far away is reaching its highest pitch since 1999, and perhaps even going back to the early '80s.

The day plays off of the Jedi blessing, “May the Force Be With You," by simply inserting “Fourth" to signify the date. Disney has actually celebrated it since the company purchased the saga's rights from Lucasfilm, and this pop culture holiday is now elevated to an official promotion on behalf of the brand. Given a love for puns in craft beer culture, and the inherent appeal of Star Wars to its core demographic of bearded thirty-somethings, it's only natural that May 4 is a day of destiny for this festival.

Tex Tubb's is marking Star Wars Day with a release party for Gourd Vader. Made by Madison's own House of Brews, it's an imperial pumpkin porter aged in Queen Jennie Whiskey barrels from the Old Sugar Distillery. This beer is liable to be most impressive, particularly in terms of its ABV. Only two sixth barrels were brewed, and they're set to be tapped at 4 p.m.

Gourd Vader isn't the first special Star Wars-themed release served at Tex Tubb's. For the 2013 edition of Madison Craft Beer Week, the taco palace served up Death Star Agave. Also from the House of Brews, that was a pale ale made with Galaxy hops and agave nectar.

Several other “May the 4th" (because, again, it's May 4th, see?!) events are planned for Madison Craft Beer Week. The most committed looks to be a party presented by Karben4 at Craftsman Table & Tap. It will feature several of the Madison brewery's standard beers, but temporarily rebranded for the festivities (“Princess Leia Luck Irish Red," “Ewok Party Amber"), and tastes of a new session IPA that'll be served in a firkin prepped with Galaxy and Comet hops. Then there's a Star Wars costume and trivia contest at the Tipsy Cow (featuring Left Hand Brewing of Longmont, Colorado), another costume contest at the Malt House (and subsequent Revenge of the Fifth party), and a brew duel at Sprecher's Restaurant & Pup (featuring what else but Sprecher Brewery of Glendale, Wisconsin).

All that's missing is a Toppling Goliath party offering Light Speed, a pale ale from that's made with Millennium and Falconer's Flight hops. Nevertheless, the festivities should leave plenty of devotees cowering in their Tauntaun sleeping bags the next day.

WEDNESDAY MAY 6

Kenny Lagers (Caribou Tavern)

Dur-dur-der-dier-daer-duuuur….. That's probably not a very clear patter, but voice those syllables with a twangy Yamaha DX7 thrum and visualize the roaring orange glows of afterburners in a San Diego sunset. Yup, it's the opening synthesizer bass line to “Danger Zone," the sweat-soaked theme song to Top Gun recorded by one Kenny Loggins and as subtle as a missile tipped with '80s tropes.

These rah-rah memories of cinematic dogfights and inverted birds are the centerpiece of the “New Belgium Kenny Lagers '80s Rockoff" at the Caribou. In a throwback to an era when macros were beer superpowers, those who have a need for speed are invited to ride this “highway to the lager zone."

On the tap list are lagers from New Belgium Brewing. Though no specific names are listed, lagers made by the Fort Collins, Colorado brewery include Shift (pale lager), Blue Paddle (pilsener), and 1554 (black lager). The bar will be playing “rocking" '80s hits on its stereo, and brewery representatives will make limited edition “Kenny Lagers" hat pressings during the party.

“Kenny Lagers" is becoming a widespread beer fest conceit. Milwaukee Beer Week featured a “Kenny Lagers Yacht Rock Party" last week, as did last year's edition of the Great American Beer Festival in Denver. But “Danger Zone" is actually a bit more relevant to contemporary music than might seem beyond its surface retro appeal. The song was composed and produced by Giorgio Moroder, the Italian synth maestro who was featured on Daft Punk's 2013 album Random Access Memories. That appearance helped spark a late career renaissance for Moroder, who is getting ready to release Déjà Vu, his first album in 30 years.

THURSDAY MAY 7

Wil-Mar Center Benefit (High Noon Saloon)

The Memorial Union Terrace claims all of the glory in Madison's outdoor beer drinking options, but the patio shared by the High Noon Saloon and Brass Ring is a popular warm-weather live music-friendly hangout in its own right.

Tallgrass Brewing looks to be a natural fit for a Madison Craft Beer Week party at this scene. The Manhattan, Kansas-based brewery holds a distinct can-oriented packaging philosophy, complete with a “canifesto": “Think of them as recyclable kegs that can fit in a backpack." It's a perspective and approach that's rapidly being adopted across the craft beer world.

The evening begins with an outdoors set by The Wells Division, a pop-folky quintet from Madison. Tallgrass cans, tallboys that'll fill a pint glass, will be on special. After the sun sets, the celebration heads indoors for performances by blues rockers The 4ontheFloor out of Minneapolis, and the Madison-based country-rockers Rodeo Bums.

There's no cover for either show, as it's all a benefit for the pantry at the Wil-Mar Neighborhood Center. Sticking with the can theme, revelers are encouraged to bring canned food to donate.

“Grab Hold of a Pair" (Silk Exotic)

Over the last year, discussion has been building about the issue of sexism in the craft beer world, and deservedly so. Never mind decades of bikini-clad advertising for the macros, breweries on the micro side of the kettle have long indulged in plenty of sleazy names and label graphics that lean on tired double entendres (including in Wisconsin). But these jokes aren't funny – they're lame, they're insulting, and they make for a dumb business strategy.

Given this context, a strip club launch party for The Twins from Lake Louie Brewing is simply just another dopey pun that shouldn't go beyond bar talk. This new-ish beer, a maibock, has actually been out for over a month and is readily available on shelves. Its name and neck label references the astrological sign of Gemini, which commences each May 21. Yes, a promotional poster for the brew features a busty pair of women perched atop Ionic columns, vaguely reminiscent of the classic Minnesota Twins logo, but that's a far cry from the icky, grope-y presentation of this event. Seriously, Lake Louie?

SATURDAY MAY 9

Hullabrew (High Noon Saloon and the Brass Ring)

This event on the High Noon and Brass Ring's shared patio functions like a mini beer festival within MCBW, with offerings from six breweries, including Lake Louie in a less disappointing context, Michigan's reliably excellent Founder's Brewing, and Sheboygan's 3 Sheeps.

While live music feels like an afterthought in much of the MCBW calendar, Hullabrew's lineup has the combined heft of Madison country outfit Brown Derby and the formerly Madison-based, now mostly Milwaukee-based garage/psych/power-pop veterans of The Midwest Beat. Sure, sometimes you have to feel a little bit bad for the acts that provide live music at mega-crowded beer events, but if anyone can tap into the crowd's giddy day-drinking energy, it's these guys.
