Madison Calendar, January 1 through 7

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
December 30, 2014 @ 11:28 am

events

Ben Willis, Sonny Knight And The Lakers, Whips, and more of the best stuff in town this week.

THURSDAY JANUARY 1 

The Transgressions, Warhawks, Deal Breakers, The DUIs. Frequency, 9 p.m.

Madison trio The Transgressions are also on the bill for Friday’s Wisconsin punk show at the Majestic, but if you’re not going to that it’s worth catching their charmingly low-lifed pop-punk songs. The band has been kind of an off-and-on thing since the release of 2009’s Waste My Time EP, but they’ve recently been recording new material at Milwaukee’s esteemed Howl Street studio with Shane Hochstetler of Call Me Lightning and Zebras. Here they’ll be sharing the bill with scuzzy local hardcore/thrash melders Warhawks, fellow Madison punk-pop outfit Deal Breakers, and Milwaukee’s The DUIs.


FRIDAY JANUARY 2 

Ben Willis, Amanda Schoofs, Brian Grimm. Good Style Shop, 8 p.m. 

Former Madisonian Ben Willis (bass), Milwaukee-based Amanda Schoofs (vocals/electronics), and Madison-based Brian Grimm (cello and a bunch of insanely intimidating Chinese stringed instruments) all have one foot in formal musical training and another stretching far off into the world of abrasive, explorative free music. At this show at Good Style Shop, the three will perform solo sets and collaborative pieces. Willis and Grimm will also be playing yet another show with their band Lovely Socialite on Saturday at Mickey’s.


Heavy Looks, Whips, Little Legend, Bron Sage. High Noon Saloon, 9 p.m.

Milwaukee band Whips’ new album, Turn It On, combines tough, thrusting rock ‘n’ roll with a sleek, slithering dissonance. Singer Ashley Smith and band give songs like “Fire Drill” a deceptive polish: They’re tightly executed, but there’s plenty of jaggedness and turmoil just under the surface. Madison band Little Legend recently sent its first full-length album off for mastering, so expect some new stuff in their set of burly yet big-hearted rock.


Masked Intruder, Direct Hit, Jetty Boys, Arms Aloft, The Transgressions, Help Desk, IDFK, Skizzwhores. Majestic, 7:30 p.m. 

The Majestic’s annual Wisconsin punk showcase once again features some reliable favorites: Madison pop-punk goofballs Masked Intruder and catchy Sheboygan rippers Jetty Boys. But be sure to look further down the bill for some younger or just less appreciated highlights, especially the gruff, spirited detonations of Eau Claire band Arms Aloft (the 2010 EP Comfort At Any Cost is a good one to start with) and grime-slathered Madison trio Skizzwhores.


SATURDAY JANUARY 3 

Dogs Of War, Keon Andre Band, DJ Vilas Park Sniper. Frequency, 9 p.m.  

The new Madison band Dogs Of War, celebrating a new EP at this show, combines the grizzled flows of MCs Tefman and Samhain Bane with modern-rock-ish elements that alternately recall Deftones and 311. The three songs on the EP can be a bit uneven—”Fire” features interludes from a distracting text-to-speech program—but there are moments when everything locks together nicely, especially during Tefman’s nimble verse on “War Cry.” In any case, we’re looking forward to seeing what Dogs Of War (along with fellow locals Fringe Character) can do with the live hip-hop band format in the new year.

Sonny Knight And The Lakers, The Jimmys, People Brothers Band, DJ Phil Money. Majestic, 8:30 p.m.

R&B singer Sonny Knight has been in and out of music since recording his first singles in the 1960s, and he returned like a firecracker on 2014’s album I’m Still Here, with his versatile Twin Cities-based outfit The Lakers. In contrast to the wild rasp of fellow senior R&B comeback Charles Bradley, Knight has maintained a graceful, clear vocal tone well into his 60s. Sure, he can let out a nice rowdy growl when he wants to, as on “Hey Girl,” but the main attraction on I’m Still Here is welcoming, youthful melody, especially on “When You’re Gone” and a great cover of Rodriguez’s “Sugar Man” (which also features one of the Lakers’ many unpredictable, slithery horn arrangements). Between bands at this show, also keep your ears open for excellent Madison DJ Phil Money, who spoke to us in a recent interview.


Netmoiré, Tontine Ensemble, Lovely Socialite. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m.

Ohio trio Netmoiré combine software manipulation with saxophone to create jagged, contorted improvisations. The live recordings on Netmoiré’s Soundcloud page capture an impressive morphing process as the band blends harmonically aggressive sax lines with distorted drum samples and a big, abstract selection of crackling electronic noise. They’ll be sharing this bill with Milwaukee-based improvisational classical group Tontine Ensemble and yet another homecoming show from jazz outfit Lovely Socialite. So it’ll be a weird and adventurous night—commence the tormentin’ o’ the regulars!


Mead & Metal Fest. High Noon Saloon, 2 p.m. 

Bos Meadery will be serving up six varieties of mead at this afternoon event, and the medieval-themed day drunk will be accompanied by folk-inflected black-metal outfit Warseid, stoner-rock piledrivers Droids Attack, and “readings of medieval skaldic poems,” among other things. The metal’s pretty solid. Try not to behead anyone?

MONDAY JANUARY 5 

Pundamonium. High Noon Saloon, 7 p.m.
