Madison calendar, December 18 through 24

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
December 17, 2014 @ 8:52 am

read

Brennan Connors & Stray Passage, prints at the Chazen, the roast of Mike Schmidt, and more in Madison this week.

THURSDAY DECEMBER 18 

Play Fair Cypher. Mr. Robert’s, 9 p.m.

Madison hip-hop duo Play Fair Cypher’s recent album I Want More flew under just about everyone’s radar this year, but MCs Vedarone and Rambunxious make a good first impression here, trading hard-bitten yet positive-minded verses over a solid set of beats from DJ Pain 1. They headline this local hip-hop bill as part of the monthly Rhyme Room series.

Chuckles For Children Comedy Benefit. High Noon Saloon, 5:30 p.m.

This benefit for Children’s Hospital of Wisconsin will feature stand-up sets from local comics Geoffrey Asmus, David Freeburg, Gena Gephart, Alan Talaga, Toler Wolfe, and Ryan Mason. 

Meadowridge Art Project Opening. Meadowridge Library, 6 p.m.

Madison artist Victor Castro and his USgathering team make sculptures and other art projects that use discarded containers and other trash products, to draw attention to society’s wasteful habits. On Thursday they’ll be presenting a new installation at the west side’s Meadowridge Library, which has been a year in the making.

Legalize It! DJ Night. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m. 

Dolores, Neens, Modern Mod, Mr. Jackson. Frequency, 8:30 p.m.

Bad Cinema: Cobra. Central Library, 6:30 p.m. 

FRIDAY DECEMBER 19 

Brennan Connors & Stray Passage, Asumaya. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m.

Madison-based sax player Brennan Connors, drummer Geoff Brady, and bassist/cellist Brian Grimm form a compelling free-jazz trio. Their sets embrace plenty of sinuous melody and conversational interplay, but can just as easily dive into minimalism and dissonance. Ever-busy Madison musician Luke Bassuener opens up with his project Asumaya, whose songs combine thumb piano, percussive loops, and politically charged chanting. Asumaya has been preparing songs for a forthcoming release, The Euphemist.


American Monotypes From The Baker/Pisano Collection. Chazen Museum Of Art, through Feb. 22.  

This new exhibit at the Chazen features original works from Georgia O’Keefe, Joseph Stella, and Mary Cassatt, capturing a period in the 19th century when American painters started taking a much greater interest in printmaking. 

Whitney Mann, Buffalo Gospel, Ernie Hendrickson. High Noon Saloon, 9 p.m. 

SATURDAY DECEMBER 20 

Digibot, New Villains, The Blowtorches. Mickey’s Tavern, 10:30 p.m.

We haven’t heard much from Madison’s long-running punk band Digibot for a while now, so it’ll be good to hear some of their over-the-top leftist piledrivers of song again at this show. Also show up for The New Villains, a newer Madison band that doesn’t have any recordings out yet but has been crafting a lot of sharp power-pop tunes featuring former members of The Midwest Beat and The Grizzlies, plus Rob Oman (of surf-rock projects Knuckel Drager and Roboman) on drums.

Variety Show: Daiquiri Renee Jones, Sonny Blood, Michael Doyle Olson, Mr. Jackson. Mother Fool’s, 8 p.m. 

It’s sadly rare that a local show reflects the sheer weird diversity of the music and art that goes on in Madison. This variety bill is a big help, featuring spoken-word artist Daiquiri Renee Jones, prolific experimental musician Sonny Blood (aka Tony Endless of Madison mainstays Drunjus and Second Family Band), short films and music videos from Madison filmmaker Michael Doyle Olson, and a set from synth-powered R&B charmer Mr. Jackson.

Zebras, Fire Retarded, Dick The Bruiser. Frequency, 10 p.m. 

Madison garage-punk outfit Fire Retarded made our top 20 Madison records of 2014 list for their diverse but reliably hard-hitting debut album, Scroggz Manor. Here they’ll be joined by a couple of Madison bands we hope to hear more from in the new year: Zebras, who’ve been working on a new album of freakish Moog-streaked metal, and catchy post-punk duo Dick The Bruiser.


MONDAY DECEMBER 22 

The Roast Of Mike Schmidt. The Argus, 8 p.m. 

Mike Schmidt’s brutal one-liners and calmly deadpan stage presence have made him one of the best stand-up comedians to come up in Madison, and he’s also done a lot of work helping other comics as an organizer of the Comedy Club on State’s weekly open mic. He’s about to move to LA, but before that he’ll be roasted by a bunch of the comedians who’ve come to know him during his seven years performing here: Alan Malaga, Jackson Jones, Nick Lynch, Ian John, Ben Taylor, Johnny Walsh, Toler Wolfe, Darryl Teske, Geoffrey Asmus, Erik Dersch, Deon Green, Heather Hangford, Aaron Haag, David Fisher, and Kevin Bozeman. Read more about Schmidt in our recent interview.

Brittsommar, Blood & Sun, William Z. Villain.High Noon Saloon, 8 p.m.

Traveling between Madison, Germany, and Sweden, Sawyer Gebauer makes sweeping, baritone-voiced folk under the name Brittsomar. He’s about to release a new split 7-inch with New York art-folk outfit Blood & Sun, who will also be performing here, as will Madison’s bizarrely menacing William Z. Villain.
