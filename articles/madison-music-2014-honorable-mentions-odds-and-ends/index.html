Madison music 2014: Honorable mentions, odds and ends

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
December 11, 2014 @ 12:58 pm

listen

Garage-rock singles, weird hip-hop releases, and more Madison music that caught our ears in 2014.

After doing my local best-of list every year I still have plenty of local releases that I want to talk about. Not in a corny “everybody gets a prize” way, but in a “this little town produces a lot of cool music” way. There’s always the stuff that just barely, agonizingly doesn’t quite make it into the top 20 Madison records of 2014 list, not to mention people who mostly released singles or collaborative works with out-of-town musicians. So, a few additional things for your consideration.

Rocket Bureau, singles

Multi-instrumentalist and power-pop production wizard Kyle Motor (The Motorz, The August Teens) did most of his music-making this past year in his basement, recording under the name Rocket Bureau. Motor handles all the instruments and vocals on the 10 Rocket Bureau songs he put out this year in a series of five Bandcamp singles. My favorites, “Silhouette” and “Tuscaloosa Woman,” show Kyle’s mastery of power-pop tropes, namely all the different ways one can combine bright, powerful guitars with lyrics about girls.

The Hussy, singles and split LP with Digital Leather

The Hussy didn’t put out a proper full-length this year (they’re working on one for release next year), but the Madison garage-rock duo had a pretty standard year of yelling things onstage, writing “NO JERKS NO SQUARES” on Facebook events, and manic studio productivity. “EZ/PZ,” released on a 7-inch single from Windian Records, is one of my favorite Hussy songs so far, featuring a great vocal from drummer Heather Hussy. The Hussy also released a split LP with Omaha synth-punk outfit Digital Leather. The Hussy’s side lovingly emulates some of Digital Leather’s grimy synth sounds, and ranges from another great Heather-led pop gem, “Happy,” to guitarist Bobby Hussy yelling about hairy asses on “Studs In Love.” Bobby Hussy also recorded a collaborative EP this year with Digital Leather member Shawn Foree, under the somewhat ill-advised name TIT.

Rob Dz, “Artwork” 

The longtime Madison rapper is still working on his first new album in 10 years, but what he’s shared from it so far is promising. He attacks the most recent single, “Artwork,” with rejuvenated swagger and with verses about the joys of creativity: “With each word I’m building me a steeple /And if you open my works up, then you’ll find my cathedral."

Calabash Boom, “Hisarna” b/w “Mbira”

Chants’ Jordan Cohen, Control drummer Luke Bassuener, and Dietrich Gosser drummer Dan Kuemmel’s first single under the name Calabash Boom finds them creating dance jams that rely solely on percussion instruments. There’s some post-production from Cohen, but no synths or outside samples. So what the trio ends up with is a hybrid of purist and experimental tendencies, with austere-sounding but still engaging results.

The Rough Guide To The Best African Music You’ve Never Heard  

This compilation, part of an extensive and well-regarded World Music Network compilation series, featured a song by Madison’s own Tani Diakite And The Afrofunkstars.

Dorval & Deveraux 

Madison’s Beau Deveraux (who made our top 20 list for his latest EP as Samantha Glass) collaborated with Vancouver musician Crystal Dorval on this set of euphorically spaced-out electronic jams.

Play Fair Cypher, I Want More

This is a good hip-hop album and I feel a bit dumb for not having written much about it yet. Madison MCs Vedarone and Rambunxious’ first album as Play Fair Cypher centers on no-nonsense but also positive and encouraging verses, plus a bunch of good beats produced by DJ Pain 1. Rambunxious’ baritone (recalling a bit of Chali 2na or Kev Roc) and Vedarone’s more mid-range approach give the whole album a great balance of contrasting vocal textures. PFC will be playing on December 18 at Mr. Roberts.

DJ Pain 1, various collaborations 

Madison’s superstar producer had another busy year, releasing a collaborative album with Sole, producing tracks for other national artists including Lil’ Bibby’s “We Are Strong,” and just hinted on Facebook that he contributed a beat to Ludacris’ new EP.

Zed Kenzo, Violently Ill 

I’m pretty sure she’s left Madison since putting this out—hard to keep track of these young folks, especially in often-transient Madison—but Zed Kenzo, formerly known as Zedd The Incredible, deserves a nod for this seven-song release of delightful bizarro hip hop. What the hell is this, and why didn’t I follow up on it? Gah.

Midas Bison, Glazer EP 

Another eccentric youngster, Sidney Johnson, aka Midas Bison, recently left Madison for Texas. While still in Madison this summer he released this three-song EP of his beamingly sincere weirdo electro-pop. It’s as disarming as it is off-the-wall, and even if it’s not your thing, you’ve got to admit that nobody else around town made music quite like this.
