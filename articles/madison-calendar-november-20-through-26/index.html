Madison calendar, November 20 through 26

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi /
Chris Lay
@TotesChris /
November 19, 2014 @ 8:51 am

read

Run The Jewels, Cronenberg, Miami Dolphins, and more of the best stuff coming to Madison this week.

(Pictured: Jennifer Jason Leigh in Existenz, screening Thursday at the Chazen.)

THURSDAY NOVEMBER 20 

Mini-Indie Film Fest. Union South Marquee, through Nov. 23. 

The WUD Film Committee moves its annual Mini-Indie Film Fest from spring to fall this time, creating an opportunity to get out of the shadow of the spring-dominating Wisconsin Film Festival. And Mini-Indie just might come into its own, with a lineup that spans from the Mark Duplass- and Elizabeth Moss-starring drama The One I Love to experimental fare like Dusty Stacks Of Mom (a playfully styled documentary about filmmaker Jodie Mack’s mother’s poster shop—this one actually screened at the 2014 Wisconsin Film Festival, but otherwise there’s not much overlap). For more highlights, read this preview from our partners at LakeFrontRow.

Cinematheque: Existenz. Chazen Museum of Art, 7 p.m. 

David Cronenberg’s 1999 film Existenz explores the immersive world video games the way 1983’s Videodrome explored the prurience of exploitative television—with a canny eye and a grisly meld of the organic with the futuristic. Jennifer Jason Leigh stars as a video game designer whose latest game uses a quasi-biological device that plugs directly into the user’s spinal cord, and leads her into head-spinning intrigue somewhere along the seam between the real and the virtual.

Brooks Wheelan. Comedy Club On State, through Nov. 22. 

Being on Saturday Night Live for a single season puts you in good company, it turns out. Gilbert Gottfried, Sarah Silverman, Ben Stiller, and Janeane Garofalo all made it but a single year before Lorne Michaels let them go for whatever arcane reasons he had at the time. We can now add Brooks Wheelan, who was “fired” earlier this year, to that list. Wheelan immediately turned those Lorne-mons into Lorne-monade and struck out on what he called "The Brooks Wheelan Falls Back on Stand Up Comedy (sorta) Tour" which, after ten or so dates across the past few months, deposits him and finely honed (we hope) material here for a weekend at the Comedy Club On State, where on Saturday he’ll be recording his debut standup album. Whelan won’t be falling back on standup for long though, since he’s currently got a Comedy Central pilot in the works with H. Jon Benjamin, another pilot in the hopper over at Adult Swim, and a role alongside fellow comedian Kurt Braunohler in The Out Of Town Wedding.

Tani Diakite And The Afrofunkstars. Overture Hall Lobby, 6 p.m. 

Legalize It! DJ Night. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m.

FRIDAY NOVEMBER 21 

Run The Jewels, Ratking, Despot, David Ruffin Theory. Majestic, 9 p.m. (sold out)

Atlanta rapper Killer Mike and NYC producer/MC El-P memorably kicked off a partnership on Killer Mike’s 2012 album R.A.P. Music, then kept going under the name Run The Jewels, releasing a free self-titled album in 2013 and Run The Jewels 2 this year. RTJ feels like the big, exuberant, obnoxious outburst both artists have deserved for a good long while: Not only is it bringing them sudden popularity after years of relatively underground toil, it’s also just clear they’re both having a shitload fun, and that comes through terrifically on both albums. That isn’t to say it waters down El-P’s warped vision or Killer Mike’s fiery politics, but it provides a welcome chance for both to loosen up a bit.


Cinematheque: Appropriate Behavior. Vilas Hall, 7 p.m. 

UW-Cinematheque presents the Madison premiere of Iranian-American writer, director, and actor Desiree Akhavan’s new comedy, in which Akhavan plays a bisexual young woman coping with modern relationships and her conservative family.

Miami Dolphins, Control, Minotaurs. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m. 

Twin Cities band Miami Dolphins make playful, disjointed punk that cares little for the difference between melody and dissonant scribbling. Beth Bambery’s vocals, though volatile in their own right, are also what holds it all together on the 2014 album Becky, which swings between goofball art-pop and abrasive punk with charming results. Sharing the bill here are the ever-solid Madison post-punk band Control and a new local garage-rock outfit The Minotaurs, who recently released their debut single, “I Won’t Compete.” 


SATURDAY NOVEMBER 22 

Count This Penny. Gates Of Heaven, 7:30 p.m. 

Madison band Count This Penny are playing two shows at the Gates to celebrate the release of their new EP, Wolves Are Sheep. (The first show, on Friday, sold out quickly.) As we noted a few weeks ago, the friendly and well-liked folk outfit hasn’t turned Hank III on us or anything, but does explore darker moods and and a more earthy sound on this EP. Founding members Amanda and Allen Rigell have always welcomed a lot of collaborators (and, in Amanda’s case, lent harmony vocals to lots of other local artists) since settling in Madison in 2010, but on Wolves Are Sheep CTP solidifies into a more cohesive band lineup with drummer Ben Wolf and lead guitarist Andrew Harrison.


Cinematheque: The Life And Death Of Colonel Blimp. Vilas Hall, 7 p.m. 

British filmmakers Michael Powell and Emeric Pressburger’s 1943 epic charts the long and frequently comic career of a British general, from the Boer War to World War II.

Wanda Sykes. Orpheum, 8 p.m. 

While we confess our favorite Wanda Sykes moments involve her laying into Larry David on Curb Your Enthusiasm, she’s also had a long career as an actor, writer, and producer (most recently executive producing the revived Last Comic Standing). As a standup, Sykes is topical and accessible but also a lot more sharp and self-possessed than your average mainstream comic.


Young Indian, Xenocom. The Wisco, 9 p.m. 

Dubuque, Iowa’s Young Indian makes instrumental rock that’s both delicate and hefty, and a lot more vulnerable and nuanced than its endearingly in-joke-y song titles (like “Nope, We’re Back To Hate” and “Thanks For Making Me Popular”) would indicate. The band plays here behind its 2014 album Handsome, with local experimental-rock weirdos Xenocom opening.


House Of Love: Colette, Anonymous, Wyatt Agard, Lovecraft. Jolly Bob’s, 10 p.m.

SUNDAY NOVEMBER 23 

Cinematheque: Topaz. Chazen Museum of Art, 2 p.m. 

Alfred Hitchcock’s 1969 film adapts Leon Uris’ sprawling novel about American, French, and Russian spies in the intrigue that led up to the Cuban Missile Crisis.

MONDAY NOVEMBER 24 

Milk Weed, Tiny Band. High Noon Saloon, 6 p.m. 

TUESDAY NOVEMBER 25 

Tom Gullion. Brink Lounge, 7:30 p.m. 

A sax and flute player who currently resides in Viroqua, Tom Gullion released his first album as a bandleader, Cat’s Cradle, in 1999. He plays here to celebrate the release of a new album, Time It Is, with David Cooper (trumpet), Tim Whalen (piano), Mark Urness (bass) and Ernie Adams (drums, percussion). The album finds Gullion and his bandmates in a warm, conversational mood, from the laid-back opening track “Message In A Bottle” to the more skronky and punchy “Mingle In The Mincing Machine.” (Oh, jazz song titles.)

WEDNESDAY NOVEMBER 26 

Kevin Bozeman, Chad Daniels, Roy Wood Jr., Mike Lebovitz. Orpheum, 8 p.m. 

Comic Kevin Bozeman hosts another of his customary Madison pre-Thanksgiving shows, with guests including the charmingly flustered Roy Wood Jr. Hannibal Buress, previously scheduled to perform, has canceled.
