Madison calendar, January 15 through 21

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi /
Joel Shanahan
@golden_donna /
January 14, 2015 @ 12:00 am

Thomas Gaudynski's drawings are at the Watrous Gallery through March 1; José James plays the Capitol Theater on Jan. 16; and Protomartyr play the High Noon Saloon on Jan. 17 as part of FRZN Fest.

events

New art at the Watrous Gallery, José James, Protomartyr, and more of the best stuff in Madison this week.

THURSDAY JANUARY 15

FRZN Fest High Noon Saloon, through Jan. 18, see link for full schedule

As with prior years, we wish this year's installment of FRZN Fest was a bit more daring (or at least surprising), but there are certainly a few serious gems to be found in the lineup. Day one standouts are young Rhymesayers associate deM atlaS, who fires off introspective couplets and anthemic pop-rock hooks over sample-laden rhythms, and Northern California-based folk-pop songstress Lia Ices. You should also probably arrive on time for an opening set from Madison's own prankster mutants Cribshitter. Day two's most interesting draw (by a landslide) would have to be Milwaukee electro-rockers GGOOLLDD, who released a very solid EP that blends retro-fitted synth-rock moods with a bit of modern pop bombast in last year's $TANDARD$. The third night might be the strongest, as it's packing a couple solidly scuzzy Detroit rock acts like the mighty Protomartyr and ex-Awesome Color member Derek Stanton's latest outfit Turn To Crime, plus the night will be punctuated by the infectious punk-pop of Cleveland's Cloud Nothings. While Saturday's show might be the strongest top to bottom, the act we're most excited for, Chicago guitar prophet Ryley Walker, is opening on Sunday for a solo set from ex-The Walkmen frontman Hamilton Leithauser.


John Roy. Comedy Club On State, through Jan. 17, see link for showtimes

It's a rare comedian who can bring the same amiable, sly touch to both a bit about why it's dumb to make fun of hipsters (“Maybe skinny pants are dumb. But you know what's dumber? Caring about pants!") and a bit about growing up with alcoholic parents (“Growing up for me was like being the manager of a very unpopular bar."). But John Roy does that and makes it sound pretty breezy and effortless on his 2013 stand-up album Alexander Hamilton, which proves there's a lot of craft tucked behind Roy's straightforward, accessible exterior.


Rhyme Room. Mr. Robert's, 9 p.m., see link for full lineup (free)

Legalize It! Mickey's Tavern, 10 p.m. (free)

FRIDAY JANUARY 16

José James. Capitol Theater, 8 p.m.

Singer José James tends toward a contemporary, genre-eluding approach on most of his solo albums—the most recent, 2014's While You Were Sleeping and 2013's No Beginning, No End, fluidly draw together elements from jazz, R&B, and hip hop. James' show at the Capitol Theater might have a more traditional feel than some of his fans are used to, though: Here, he'll be performing his interpretations of Billie Holiday tunes, in preparation for an upcoming Holiday-centered record called Yesterday I Had The Blues. Following the show, Madison's New Breed jazz band and MC/spoken-word artist Rob Dz will be performing in Overture's Promenade Hall.


Art Opening: Thomas Gaudynski: Object Syntax and Brandon Norsted: The Purchase of Deep Water. Watrous Gallery, 5:30 p.m. (free; exhibits run through March 1)

Milwaukee artist Thomas Gaudynski and Madison's Brandon Norsted both play with the forms and roles of household objects in new side-by-side solo exhibitions at the Watrous Gallery. Gaudynski's drawings pile together groups of various things—guitars, dolls, furniture—in a way that comes off as both playful and disorienting. Norsted also takes everyday objects out of context in mixed-media sculptures that reference furniture and woodwork.

Paint, Lover's Spit, Whale House, Ming Kurray. Mickey's Tavern, 10 p.m. (free)

Paint is one of many solid youngster garage-punk bands that have popped up in Madison over the past couple of years, making a promising debut with last year's raw, scraggly Wet Paint EP. They'll . Madison band Lover's Spit had a show booked at Mickey's last week, which we ran a brief preview of, but they had to cancel their set due to a burst pipe, so just a reminder to check out their amiably frenetic indie-rock tunes here.


SATURDAY JANUARY 17

How It Feels Zine Release Party. Mickey's Tavern, 9:30 p.m. (free)

Madison artist and zine publisher Claire Warhus (who drew something awesome for us a while back) is throwing this party to celebrate the second issue of How It Feels, a zine dedicated to realistic, funny, humiliating, and even sweet stories about sex. For this issue, Warhus asked contributing writers and artists to submit things about sexual firsts. The first issue came festooned with Warhus' illustration of rampaging genitalia, so expect the zine to capture a spirit of both sex-positive openness and inner-12-year-old crudeness.

SUNDAY JANUARY 18

Greater Madison Jazz Consortium Benefit. Cardinal Bar, 6 p.m.

The non-profit Greater Madison Jazz Consortium was responsible for last year's successful Strollin' mini-jazz-fest series [http://www.tonemadison.com/articles/the-strollin-jazz-series-promising-model/], which it hopes to bring back this year while also pursuing a number of musical education and community-building activities. Donations at the door to this show, in addition to support from grants and local businesses, will support the group's agenda for this year—which includes starting up yet another performance series featuring local musicians, setting up a grant fund to support local jazz artists, and creating a new Madison jazz website with content including daily show listings. Trombone player Darren Sterud will lead his big band, the Darren Sterud Orchestra, which by itself offers a pretty convincing cross-section of how many talented jazz musicians are lurking in Madison. The New Breed, who hold down a great residency on Tuesday nights at the Cardinal, will perform with Rob Dz and invite other MCs and spoken-word artists to join them. Madison jazz guitarist Charlie Painter will perform here in a trio format.

WEDNESDAY JANUARY 21

Ignite Madison: Music Matters. High Noon Saloon, 8 p.m.

This installment of the Ignite Madison short-talks series focuses on music, with proceeds from admission going to Madison Music Makers, an organization that works to bring more music education to low-income and minority students. The night will center on five-minute presentations from musicians and other music-involved folk, including luthier Adam Steinquist; folk artist Anna Vogelzang; violinist, vocalist and educator Alida LaCosse; and DJ Radish and Dudley Noon of New Nature Collective.

Particle Fever. Union South Marquee, 7 p.m. (free)

Mark Levinson's 2013 documentary Particle Fever (which played at last year's Wisconsin Film Festival) follows the scientists involved in the Large Hadron Collider and its ultimately triumphant search for a particle called the Higgs Boson. The film makes the science accessible and exciting through interviews and some stellar visualizations (Levinson holds a doctorate in particle physics and producer David Kaplan is a theoretical-physics professor at Johns Hopkins), but ultimately it succeeds because it focuses so well on the people behind the particle search, closely following six different scientists through the suspense, rewards, and frustrations of discovery.
