The Mallards’ 2015 promotional schedule is goddamn insane again

Chris Lay
@TotesChris
April 21, 2015 @ 11:54 am

Rowdy Roddy Piper, seen here in "They Live," visits the Mallards on June 27.

events

Rowdy Roddy Piper, bacon and all manner of bobble-things descend upon the Duck Pond this season.

Lots of people go to Madison Mallards games for the baseball. They're consistently a great team, having made it to the Northwoods League playoffs in six of the past 15 years, and they even won the championship as recently as 2013. That said, as the team rolls out its 2015 promotions schedule, we're reminded of the undeniable truth that many more people go to Mallards games for the all-you-can-eat-and-drink “Duck Blind" area, the crazy themed food nights (separate nights celebrating bacon AND hot dogs!), and the undeniable magnetism of anything even loosely described as a “bobblehead."

Let's get that one out of the way first: For bobbles this year, you get the “Marshfield Clinic Soccer Millie" (May 28), the “Pepsi Outdoorsman Maynard" (June 19), and the “Zimbrick Honda Firefighter Maynard" (June 28), among others, but the real intriguing ones that really trip off the tip of the tongue are the “Dentistry for Madison: Dr. Jay Hazen Hippy vs Hipster Maynard" (June 12) where you get to choose either “Hippy" or “Hipster" variants ( YOU MUST PICK ONE), and the “Great Dane Mohawk Man bobble mohawk" (July 24) which... Is it a bobble with a mohawk? Is it a wearable mohawk that... bobbles? The mind reels.

While the Mallards organization clearly hasn't lost its affinity for nutty gimmicks, it does seem to be scaling back the “Celebrity Appearances" ever so slightly this year. 2014 brought us Diamond Dallas Page teaching yoga, the “ Jump To Conclusions Mat" guy from Office Space, and “postmodern Internet clown" Jose Canseco hitting juiced-up dingers. This year we get WWE Hall Of Famer, star of the films They Live (!) and Hell Comes To Frogtown (?), and occasional stand-up comedian Rowdy Roddy Piper (June 27), as well as Shane Obedzinski, who played Tommy "Repeat" Timmons in The Sandlot (July 2). (The night Piper visits is not one of the Mallards' "Quench Gum Giveaway" nights, so it's more than feasible for fans to be, ahem, out of bubblegum.)

Thursday, June 4 is “ Star Wars Night," in which Mallards players will wear R2-D2 jerseys that will then be auctioned off during the game. I'm in, but only if they make the opponents wear IG-88 jerseys that are also auctioned off. No clue what the auction is supposed to benefit, but might I suggest the Watto-Defamation League? I hope that next year, we'll see Maynard as BB-88 (where the BB stands for “bobble," of course)!

For the adventurous foodies among us, Friday, June 5 is “Bacon Appreciation Night." As someone who's attended the past few years of bacon night, I can tell you that the Mallards do not fuck around on bacon night. Past menu items have included deliciously artery-hardening fare such as bacon tacos, bacon burgers on glazed donuts, “bacon beer battered bacon-wrapped pigsicles"(?!), and, for dessert of course, bacon chocolate sundaes.

If you happen to schedule your trip to a Mallards game on a rare night that doesn't feature a bonkers promotion, listen for the announcer casually saying “weiner" over the mic, because you'll still be able to turn in any foul balls you catch for a hot dog in one of the team's evergreen deals.
