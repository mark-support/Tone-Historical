Tone Madison’s Free Comic Book Day 2015 cheat sheet

Chris Lay
@TotesChris
May 01, 2015 @ 9:33 am

read

"Hip-Hop Family Tree," a "Bob’s Burgers" comic, and more stuff you might want to grab this Saturday.

Since its inception in 2002, Free Comic Book Day has found itself paired with a number of comic book-based blockbuster movies, from greats like Avengers and Iron Man to bombs like X-Men Origins: Wolverine and The Amazing Spider-Man 2. This year it coincides with the release of Avengers: Age Of Ultron, but there's more to FCBD than an added marketing boost for the films. Lots of smaller indie companies use the day to get the word out on properties that may never end up on the big screen, and a number of kid-friendly publishers are looking to get their books into the hands of the next generation of comic fans. Here are a few we're looking forward to picking up, but there are dozens of comics to be had, so by all means get out there and try a bunch on for size.

Where do you get the comics? Westfield Comics has two locations in Madison ( 7475 Mineral Point Rd. and 944 Williamson St.), and Capital City Comics is at 1910 Monroe Street.

2000 AD Special

The legendary British anthology comic series 2000 AD was where a great many artists and writers cut their pulpy teeth, including Alan Moore, Grant Morrison, Mark Millar, Carlos Ezquerra, Dave Gibbons, and Frank Quitely among others. Here you get “48 pages of sci-fi lunacy" to digest before it digests you. This issue is rated at “Teen+" but I assume the stories contained between its covers will be appropriately brain-rotting.

Avengers #1

C'mon. The movie opens this weekend. How are you not gonna add this to your Free Comic Book Day pile? How? “Everyone's favorite heroes take on a dangerous new threat!" You guys! A dangerous new threat!

Bob's Burgers

I just spent way too much time racking my brain for a good Burger of the Day pun based on “Free Comic Book Day" and I came up with nothing. Leek Corn-mic Bacon Day (leeks, corn, bacon)? Cheese Hominy Book-a-DAYga (cheese, hominy, rutabaga)? Those are terrible. Come up with your own and show me how it's done? Please?

Comic Book Legal Defense Fund

The CBLDF does great work protecting the First Amendment rights of creators as well as doing its best to expand the readership of the medium, so its annual Free Comic Book Day books are always worth picking up. This year it's a collection highlighting stories targeted at young adults, so snatch one up to pass along to an interesting kiddo or other in your life.

Fight Club / The Goon / The Strain

Ah, Fight Club. “I want you to hit me as hard as you can" and Tyler Durden and the whatnot. You've read the book and you've seen the movie, and now you can read the comic book! Chuck Palahniuk is on board as the writer of this, the “premiere" of the graphic novel adaptation of his book, but if that's not doing it for you, it's also got new stories from Eric Powell's The Goon and Guillermo del Toro's The Strain, which are labeled as being for “Mature Readers." Dig that David Mack cover art!

Hip Hop Family Tree 3-in-1

Ed Piskor's incredibly well-sourced and remarkably fun Hip Hop Family Tree, which breaks down the early roots of hip-hop, is my infotainment happy place. This funky little 3-in-1 freebie is the perfect thing to entice newbies to the series and fill the void for the faithful until Vol. 3 comes out later this year.

Lady Justice

“Back in print for the first time in two decades!" It's a long-forgotten Neil Gaiman tale, what else do you want? I think I still have the chromium trading card promoting this that was slipped into the polybag of an issue of Wizard Magazine somewhere, actually.

Supermutant Magic Academy Step Aside Pops Combo

Drawn & Quarterly always puts out interesting stuff, and this is a sampling of highly anticipated upcoming works from Kate Beaton ( Hark! A Vagrant) and Jillian Tamaki (This One Summer) is suitable for all ages so dig in!

The Tick

Spoon! Whether you remember the idiosyncratic mid-'90s cartoon, the misunderstood early-aughts live action sitcom, or you're an original gangster for the late-'80s comic books, pretty much everyone who's encountered The Tick has fallen at least a little bit in love with the lunky do-gooder.
