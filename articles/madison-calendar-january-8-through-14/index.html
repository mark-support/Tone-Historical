Madison calendar, January 8 through 14

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
January 07, 2015 @ 9:00 am

Patton Oswalt plays the Orpheum on Jan. 14, Proud Parents play Mickey's on Jan. 9, and Luke Arvid plays Crescendo on Jan. 9.

read

Patton Oswalt, Proud Parents, Luke Arvid, and more of the best stuff in Madison this week.

THURSDAY JANUARY 8 

Naqoyqatsi. Central Library, 6:30 p.m. 

Godfrey Reggio’s 2002 film Naqoyqatsi forms a documentary trilogy (along with 1982’s Koyaanisqatsi and 1988’s Powaqqatsi) that uses wordless footage and Philip Glass compositions to lament the excesses and deprivations of a modern, globalized world. Where Koyaanisqatsi showed the industrialized world at odds with nature and Powaqqatsi chronicled third-world cultures endangered by modern forces, Naqoyqatsi examines the pervasive role of technology, portraying it as a violent force pitted against human life.

Drunk Drivers, The Garza, The Skintones, The Mersey Brothers. Frequency, 8 p.m.

Four Wisconsin bands are throwing this show to benefit a friend who is battling leukemia. It’s especially worth turning out for Madison noise-rock trio The Garza and veteran Eau Claire miscreants Drunk Drivers.

FRIDAY JANUARY 9 

Luke Arvid, Twin Brother. Crescendo, 7:45 p.m. 

Madison singer-songwriter Luke Arvid and Milwaukee band Twin Brother should pair up nicely on this bill of weary, thoughtfully textured folk. Twin Brother’s 2014 album Swallow The Anchor has a good bit of ruggedness to go with its wistful melodies, thanks to darkly spacious production and Sean Raasch’s vocals, which recall Crooked Fingers’ Eric Bachmann (or the more restrained moments of Frog Eyes’ Carey Mercer). Luke Arvid made a more sonically sparse but melodically rich debut with 2013’s album Arc, and is currently working on a follow-up for release in February.


Jerry Seinfeld. Overture Hall, 7 (sold out) & 9:30 p.m. 

Jerry Seinfeld’s minutiae-fueled stand-up style is easy to mock (“and whu-u-uut’s the deal with…”) but classic in its lighter-than-air way. And Seinfeld’s breezy flippancy has aged surprisingly well in more recent stand-up sets, and his instinct for mixing the trivial with comic cleverness has translated nicely in the Comedians In Cars Getting Coffee web series. Whether this means he’s $70-plus good, well, you’ll have to let us know.

Lover’s Spit, Proud Parents, Heavy Looks. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m.

Proud Parents is a newer Madison band whose power-pop songs focus on the charming chemistry of singer/guitarists Tyler Fassnacht and Claire Nelson-Lifson, and it also features The Hussy’s Heather Hussy on drums. Fellow Madison band Lover’s Spit blast out frenetic, blurry tunes that draw on wiry guitar hooks and propulsive drums.


Night Light: Madison Storytellers. Central Library, 8 p.m. 

For the Madison Public Library’s grown-up/after-hours Night Light series, the Madison Storytellers will be hosting an evening of Moth-style true storytelling centered on the theme of rivalry (go to the link for info on how to sign up). The night will also celebrate new art exhibits at the Central Library by Kimberly Benson, Poonam Rao, and Craig Grabhorn.

SATURDAY JANUARY 10 

Faux Fawn, Anna Vogelzang, Dolores, Simon Balto, Christopher Gold. Majestic, 8:30 p.m.

We’ve had a lot to say recently about Madison/Stoughton band Faux Fawn, who play this show behind last year’s brooding album Lonesome Loon. The band’s versatile chamber-folk dynamic and Paul Otteson’s raspy, high-register vocals are easily the most interesting things on this bill of Wisconsin bands, which also features Madison singer-songwriter Anna Vogelzang and whimsical local indie-pop act Dolores.

MONDAY JANUARY 12 

Stone Titan, Satans Dealer, Blunt, No Question. The Wisco, 9 p.m. 

Connecticut band Stone Titan elevate (or degrade?) doom metal to an exuberant splatter of grimy riffs and shuddering, tremolo-picked, wah-pedaled noise on the 2013 album Scratch ‘N’ Sniff. The band has plenty of solid riffs and blown-out low end, but there’s an unmistakable playfulness in the band’s execution, and in song titles like “A Brief History Of Overweight Men.” This show also features a new Madison hardcore band, No Question.


TUESDAY JANUARY 13 

Vid Libert, Matt Joyce. Mickey’s Tavern, 10 p.m. 

When we last checked in with singer-songwriter and dub-psych experimenter Vid Libert, he had just finished an album under the name Kola Gong (one of our top 20 Madison records of 2014) and was leaving his hometown of Madison for a period of indefinite travel. But he’s back in town and playing a couple last-minute shows before hitting the road again. Along with Libert’s earnest folk tunes, the night will feature a solo set from The Midwest Beat’s Matt Joyce.

WEDNESDAY JANUARY 14 

Patton Oswalt. Orpheum, 8 p.m. 

In the decade between his first stand-up album, 2004’s Feelin’ Kinda Patton, and his fifth, last year’s Comedy Plus Tragedy Equals Time, Patton Oswalt has scored some mainstream success in film and TV (Ratatouille, Big Fan, Justified, etc.), gained respect as a social and cultural commentator even outside of his comedy, and published two memoirs, Zombie Spaceship Wasteland and the new Silver Screen Fiend. And yet he’s no less the geeky, excoriating, surreal comic we first flipped-out for, but just an increasingly well-rounded, actualized, grounded version of same. Comedy Plus Tragedy Equals Time isn’t really more tame than Feelin’ Kinda Patton or 2007’s masterpiece Werewolves And Lollipops, just invested with a perspective that’s rare in any corner of the stand-up world.
