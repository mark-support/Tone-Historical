A choosy guide to Fall Gallery Night 2014

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
October 06, 2014 @ 8:08 am

read

Skip the knick-knacks and find the interesting stuff at Gallery Night on Friday.

(Pictured: Yvette Marie Dostatni's 'Conventioneers' photos.) Madison's twice-yearly Gallery Night, set for Friday, October 10, can be a gauntlet of knick-knacks or a stimulating evening of art, depending on the path you've take. We've picked out some of the most promising Gallery Night events in partnership with our friends at Madison art site Spackle Madison. For the full schedule, visit the Madison Museum of Contemporary Art's website. Not all of our picks are on the official Gallery Night schedule, so click the links for more info.

PhotoMidwest: Yvette Marie Dostatni: Conventioneers

A quick browse through the 'Conventioneers' section on photographer Dostatni's website yields up straight-on, captivating looks at obscure industries and subcultures and the people who inhabit them. The subjects, from dry cleaners to Abraham Lincoln impersonators, come through in rich but startlingly frank black-and-white.

Jack Long: A Brief Moment Of Perfection (Memorial Union Lakefront On Langdon Gallery)

Milwaukee-based photographer Jack Long has described his work as "fluid sculptures," which is accurate in that his high-speed shots capture the strange shapes that liquids form as they spray, splash, and collide. Often he'll incorporate several bodies of liquid, each with a different color, to add yet another dimension to these fleeting formations.

Art Lofts

The Arts Lofts can be a bit hard to find if you're not involved in UW-Madison's art programs, but if you schlep over to this space near the Kohl Center, you'll often find intriguing, short-running exhibitions from UW students and faculty. On Gallery Night, the lofts will be featuring art from UW-Madison's adjunct art lecturers.

Studio Paran: Richard Jones: What Is The Smallest Inhabitable Space?

On Gallery Night, this working glass studio reliably fills up with visitors eager to check out Richard Jones' glass-blowing demonstrations. But it's usually just as worthwhile for the art Jones creates, set aside from the vases and other admittedly beautiful glass products. Jones is adept at creating meditative, abstract little worlds from glass orbs and other forms, sometimes hanging on the studio's walls and sometimes dangling from the ceiling.

Tandem Press

Tandem is a bit out of the way but is one of Madison's most formidable visible-art institutions. A printmaking operation affiliated with UW-Madison, it boasts an ever-growing body of work from an ever-expanding group of guest artists. Of special note on Gallery Night should be works from Allison Saar and from Irish artist Maser.

Afterparty with Rob Dz (MMOCA, 9 p.m.)

Once Gallery Night proper winds down, Madison MC and spoken-word artist Rob Dz will perform in the MMOCA lobby. Rob is currently at work on a new album, 'The Good Guy Memoirs,' and his sharp and playful presence should be a fun way to close out the evening. There is a $5 cover for non-MMOCA members.

Madison Art Hub (78 N. Bryan St.)

One of the more far-flung locations on this year's Gallery Night list, the Madison Art Hub is a new multi-artist gallery and workspace in an industrial building just off Milwaukee Street.

La Luz y La Sombra (The Light and The Shadow) at Edgewood Galler

Edgewood College's gallery is currently hosting an excellent show in which artist Carolyn Kallenborn explores Dia de los Muertos. It includes a film and installation by Kallenborn as well as more than 100 personal altars of remembrance and celebration made by Madison community members.

Little Galleries

Chele Isaac, whose multi-disciplinary work infuses installation art with video elements, is doing a miniature projection installation in the Little Mifflin Gallery (1921 E. Mifflin St.). In the Little Monroe Gallery (1901 Monroe St.), Melanie Kehoss is creating a paper cut installation based on the history of Lake Wingra. These aren't on the official Gallery Night schedule. (Full disclosure: Our partners at Spackle also include the Little Galleries curators.)
