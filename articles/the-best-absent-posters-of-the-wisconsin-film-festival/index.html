The best (absent) posters of the Wisconsin Film Festival

Chris Lay
@TotesChris
March 13, 2015 @ 8:52 am

watch

WFF doesn’t often get to show off posters, so we picked 15 striking ones for this year’s films.

One of the very few sad things about the Wisconsin Film Festival is that the art of movie posters gets lost in the shuffle. The still images you see in WFF's handy annual guide rarely offer many clues as to the aesthetics of the films, nor are they too terribly persuasive in most cases. Given the thought and occasional burst of stand-alone brilliance that goes into movie posters, that's a bit of a shame (if understandable on a logistical level—imagine curating 160-odd titles and trying to round up individual promotional materials for each, and you realize that's actually asking a bit much). In hopes of making up for it just a little bit, consider our 15 favorite posters that accompany WFF 2015 feature selections. You might at least think, “well, that looks cool," and perhaps you'll end up checking out a flick you otherwise would have written off. Know any you think should've made the cut? Link to them in the comments!
