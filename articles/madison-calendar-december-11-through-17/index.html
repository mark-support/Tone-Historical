Madison calendar, December 11 through 17

Scott Gordon
@scottgordonwi
December 10, 2014 @ 12:00 am

read

The Sea And Cake, Oedipus Tex, Wintersong, and more of the best stuff in Madison this week.

(Pictured: The Sea And Cake. Photo by Jim Newberry.)

THURSDAY DECEMBER 11

The Sea And Cake, Building On Buildings. High Noon Saloon, 8 p.m.

Chicago’s The Sea And Cake has spent 20 years crafting and perfecting one of the most distinctive sounds in contemporary rock. As of their latest album, 2012’s Runner, that sound was still effervescent, snappy, with room for ever-intriguing little details to seep in. Live, the sound tends to pick up a little more bass and punch. Madison’s Building On Buildings, who recently put out a solid debut album, open the show.


Nashville Pussy, Valient Thorr, Against The Grain. Majestic, 9 p.m.

FRIDAY DECEMBER 12 

Oedipus Tex, Jeremiah Nelson. Nutty Bar at Bandung, 9:30 p.m.

Two Madison singer-songwriter projects with eccentric tendencies share this intimate bill. Oedipus Tex, a solo project by El Valiente guitarist Eric Caldera, will be playing at least one or two new songs in addition to selections from his moody and understated 2012 album Borracho Corazon. Jeremiah Nelson, who has been exploring a lot of electronic and textural experiments alongside his more song-oriented material. He’s working on a new album to follow this year’s EP Whittier, and will also be trying out some new material here.


Ghost Town Council. High Noon Saloon, 5:30 p.m. 

Madison band Ghost Town Council plays charmingly rugged Americana that’s not above a bit of swinging fun and draws on both well-worn and contemporary folk and country influences. Here, the band celebrates its recently released debut album, simply titled GTC.


Cinematheque: Meet Me In St. Louis. Vilas Hall, 7 p.m. 

Judy Garland stars in Vincente Minnelli’s 1944 movie musical, set in St. Louis around the time of the World’s Fair in 1903. Here, the film screens in 35mm, and Cinematheque founder Lea Jacobs will give an introduction.

Monty Python’s Life Of Brian. Union South Marquee, 11 p.m. http://wudfilm.com/now-playing/ ...even if they did say Jehovah!

Destroy All Christmas. Inferno, 6 p.m. 

Madison sludge-slingers Droids Attack bring back something of an annual tradition with their 10th annual Christmas party. Combining Droids’ love of heavy music and video-game dorkery, the evening will feature metal and punk bands, pizza, and Droids’ goofy Santa-Bot Mascot. Music-wise, highlights include a headlining set from Droids themselves and one from Madison noise-rock standouts Sinking Suns.

Metabaque, Fringe Character, DJ Phil Money. Rathskeller, 9 p.m.  

Madison’s Metabaque uses a big, chanting group of percussionists to pound out a mix of Brazilian rhythms, hip-hop, and other danceable styles. The band plays here to celebrate the release of a new CD, Make It Go.

SATURDAY DECEMBER 13 

Wintersong. Barrymore, 8 p.m. 

Now in its third year, Wintersong brings together a bunch of local musicians in a holiday-themed show that raises money for Second Harvest Food Bank. The bill is heavy on Madison indie-folk standbys like Anna Vogelzang, Faux Fawn, and Count This Penny. Other highlights will include Dietrich Gosser reading The Night Before Christmas while local throat-singer/noisemaker DB Pedersen provides foley sound effects.

The Kissers. High Noon Saloon, 5 and 9 p.m.  

The long-running Madison band holds two shows to celebrate a new album, Three Sails.

Counting Crows, Twin Forks. Orpheum, 8 p.m. (sold out) 

Direct Hit, Pears, Foreign Lawns. Dragonfly Lounge, 9 p.m. 

SUNDAY DECEMBER 14 

Cinematheque: Family Plot. Chazen Museum of Art, 2 p.m.

Cinematheque’s epic two-semester Hitchcock series ends with his final feature, 1976’s Family Plot, starring Bruce Dern and Barbara Harris as a couple who find themselves investigating a kidnapping plot.

WEDNESDAY DECEMBER 17 

Arts Happy Hour. Bright Red Studios, 5:30 p.m. 

Bridget Maniaci’s campaign for mayor of Madison is hosting this event, billed as a community discussion about what the City of Madison can do to better support the arts. Here’s hoping it turns out to be more than simply a campaign event aimed at buttering up young and creative folks, because there are all kinds of opportunities for city officials to improve their leadership on the arts front.
